\begin{frame}[fragile]\frametitle{Syntonising RF SOCs on White Rabbit}

\begin{itemize}
	\item White Rabbit provides timing for systems of interest, generally by feeding them clocks derived from the main WR clock.
	\item Assumes that no frequency approximation is done in the system of interest (wrong in the AD9361 RFSoC case),
	\item Cannot grant phase synchronisation because of PLL indeterminism.
\end{itemize}

	\end{center}

\end{frame}

\begin{frame}[fragile]\frametitle{PLL indeterminism}
\includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{pll_indeterminism_fig.pdf}

\begin{minipage}[t]{\linewidth}
\begin{minipage}{.62\linewidth}
{\footnotesize
\begin{itemize}
	\item PLL multiplying by 1.5, division by 2 yields 2 possible phase positions.
	\item Sampling these 2 possible signals at the same time would give 2 patterns: \texttt{10} or \texttt{01}
	\item A PLL with divisor N gives N patterns each N bits long. 
        \item With a LUT we know what position we are in and we can then correct it\footnote{\url{https://wiki.confluence.nikhef.nl/wiki/spaces/WRNIK/pages/934182916/Lock+Sweep}}\footnote{
K. Asteriou, P. Jansweijer, {\em LockSweep (on BabyWR) status and progress}, 14th White Rabbit
Workshop (2025) at \url{https://indico.cern.ch/event/1524513/contributions/6556312/}}: Nikhef's {\bf locksweep}
	\item By simulating the sampling of the patterns, we can {\bf automatically generate the LUT for given frequencies}
\end{itemize}}
\end{minipage}
\begin{minipage}{.47\linewidth}
\hspace*{-0.1cm}\includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{plt_pll_lock_possibilities.pdf}

\vspace{-.4cm}
{\footnotesize \hspace*{.9cm}A PLL dividing by 5 yields 5 possibilities}
\end{minipage}
\end{minipage}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}[fragile]\frametitle{Auxiliary locksweep}

\begin{itemize}
	\item Auxiliary soft PLLs cost an extra VCO, and offer fine phase control over a 62.5 MHz feedback clock.
	\item This solves the frequency approximation issue,
	\item Using the phase sampler and fine phase control, we can correct the PLL indeterminism.
\end{itemize}

	\item at the cost of poor phase noise, even at low frequencies (uncleanable).

	\item Where does it irremediably affect White Rabbit?

\begin{frame}[fragile]\frametitle{DDMTD aliasing}

\begin{minipage}{.49\linewidth}
\begin{centering}
\includegraphics[width=.7\linewidth]{ddmtd_schem.pdf}
\end{centering}

{%\footnotesize
\begin{itemize}
	\item DDMTD sub-sample the clock at $2^{15}$x Nyquist frequency
	\item this alias the high frequency of the clock to a low frequency, which's phase can be measured by a timer.
	\item The broadband digital gates also alias phase noise and spurs into the DDMTD bandwith (1.9~kHz)\footnote{Rizzi, Mattia \& Lipinski, Maciej \& Wlostowski, Tomasz \& Serrano, Javier \& Daniluk, Grzegorz \& Ferrari, P. \& Rinaldi, Stefano. (2016). White rabbit clock characteristics. 1-6. 10.1109/ISPCS.2016.7579514.}
\end{itemize}}
\end{minipage}
\begin{minipage}{.49\linewidth}
\includegraphics[width=.9\linewidth]{freq_by_spur_1k8k.pdf}

	{\footnotesize A spur at any frequency offset is folded into a low frequency spur, much harder to filter out.\par}

\includegraphics[width=.9\linewidth]{freq_by_spur_100k108k.pdf}
\end{minipage}

\end{frame}

\begin{frame}[fragile]\frametitle{DDMTD aliasing}

{%\footnotesize
\begin{itemize}
	\item The same amount of noise is aliased into the DDMTD bandwidth
	\item Increasing the bandwidth proportionaly lowers the noise floor

$\Rightarrow$ DDMTD integrates all the oscillator noise in the soft PLL phase detection, 
degrading low frequency phase noise.

\begin{frame}[fragile]\frametitle{Noise induced by MMCM phase shifts aliased in the DDMTD}
\begin{minipage}{.49\linewidth}
\includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{dmtd_spurs_by_mmcm_freq.png}
\end{minipage}
\begin{minipage}{.49\linewidth}
\begin{itemize}
	\item MMCM phase shifts produce phase noise spurs
	\item These spurs offsets from 62.5~MHz are proportionnal to the offset frequency generated by the phase shifts
	\item Since their positions are predictable, could they be selectively filtered ?
	\item Else, can we use less phase shifting MMCMs ?
\end{itemize}
\end{minipage}

Spectrum of the DDMTD output in the M2SDR depending on the MMCM generated frequency offset.

\end{frame}
